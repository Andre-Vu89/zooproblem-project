Zoo Animals in San Diego

Bears

Bears are powerful mammals known for their large bodies, stocky build, and distinctive fur. They are typically found in forests and mountainous regions across the world. Bears are omnivorous, feeding on a variety of foods including berries, fish, and small mammals. They are known for their hibernation habits during the winter months.

Giraffes

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, characterized by their long necks and legs. They inhabit savannas and open woodlands in Africa, using their height to browse leaves and shoots from tall trees. Their distinctive coat pattern helps them blend into their environment and provides some protection against predators.

Lions

Lions are majestic big cats known for their golden fur and loud roars. They are apex predators, living in prides across Africa's savannas and grasslands. Lions are highly social animals, with females doing most of the hunting while males defend territory and the pride. They symbolize strength and courage in many cultures.

Monkeys

Monkeys are primates known for their agility, intelligence, and social behavior. They inhabit diverse habitats from tropical forests to mountains and savannas across the world. Monkeys have prehensile tails or opposable thumbs that help them grasp objects and move through trees. They exhibit complex social structures and communication skills within their groups.

Alligators

Alligators are large reptiles belonging to the crocodilian family, known for their broad snouts and armored bodies. They primarily inhabit freshwater environments such as swamps, rivers, and lakes in the southeastern United States and China. Alligators are carnivorous predators, feeding on fish, birds, and mammals near waterways. They are also known for their ability to regulate body temperature through basking in the sun.
